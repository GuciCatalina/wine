Anthony Road Wine Company - Seneca Lake

Founded in 1990 by The Martini Family. Ann and John Martini moved to Yates County in 1973 to grow grapes and raise their family on 100 acres overlooking Seneca Lake. Their children, Peter, Sarah, Maeve and Elizabeth, have been involved in the vineyard and winery throughout their growing years and are now full-time employees/co-owners of Anthony Road Wine Company. The location of the vineyards in close proximity to Seneca Lake lends itself to a favorable microclimate for grape growing. Cooling in the summer and a few degrees above average temperatures during the winter.

1020 Anthony Rd, Penn Yan, NY 14527

Boundary Breaks - Seneca Lake

Boundary Breaks is at the very tip of the “Banana Belt” on the east shore of Seneca Lake. The winery is the creation of Bruce Murray who in 2007 purchased a 120-acre farm on the East side of Seneca Lake that had been previously focused on growing corn and soybeans. They focus on producing the finest Riesling, Cabernet Franc, and Gewurztraminer. The name, “Boundary Breaks” refers to the gorges on the northern and southern edges of our vineyards.

1568 Porter Covert Rd, Lodi, NY 14860

Chateau Lafayette Reneau - Seneca Lake

The property now Chateau LaFayette Reneau was historically a fruit farm. Chateau LaFayette Reneau took root after Dick and Betty Reno vacationed in the Finger Lakes. Falling in love with the area, they retired to the beautiful region of upstate New York. In 1985 they purchased an old brick farmhouse on a 140-acre farm located on the southeastern slopes of Seneca Lake. The Renos replaced the existing vineyards, comprised primarily of native grape varieties (Catawba, Niagara, and Concord), with fine wine grapes. They re-planted 50 acres of grapes. Chardonnay , Cabernet Sauvignon, Cabernet Franc, Malbec, Merlot, Petite Verdot, Pinot Noir, Syrah, and Seyval Blanc. By 1986, Chateau LaFayette Reneau had its first vintage and the winery was open for business.

5081 NY-414, Hector, NY 14841

Damiani Wine Cellars - Seneca Lake

Situated on the eastern shore of Seneca Lake in the heart of the Finger Lakes wine region, Damiani Wine Cellars is committed to producing bold and beautifully nuanced wines that evoke the terroir of the region

4704 NY-414, Burdett, NY 14818

Domaine Le Seurre - Keuka Lake

Céline and Sébastien LeSeurre are a French husband and wife winemaking team living and working in the Finger Lakes. When they first moved to New York, they explored the Finger Lakes by visiting and sampling grapes from the many different terroirs — geography, geology, and climate of a certain place — and soon determined this is the best place to grow beautiful grapes and craft fine wine together. They unite their French heritage and new world wine experience to develop Finger Lakes wines that pair well with fine cuisine as well as casual fare.

13920 NY-54, Hammondsport, NY 14840

Hermann J. Wiemer - Seneca Lake

Hermann J. Wiemer Vineyard is a pioneer of viticulture and winemaking in the Finger Lakes. Founded in 1979 by German-born winemaker Hermann J. Wiemer, the estate has gained national and international attention for the quality of its wines and the soundness of its growing practices.

3962 NY-14, Dundee, NY 14837

Heron Hill Winery - Keuka Lake

The Hunt family has been farming west of Keuka Lake in New York’s Finger Lakes region for seven generations. In 1981 they started crafting wine. They are committed to responsible farming and caring for the land. They have reduced their fossil fuel use by installing award-winning solar and geothermal systems. They’re improving soil health through the use of mulch and compost.

4021 Italy Hill Rd, Branchport, NY 14418

Hosmer - Cayuga Lake

Hosmer Winery was founded on the principle that great wine starts in the vineyard. Stewardship and farmland preservation were among the original goals upon purchase of the farm in the late 1920’s, and those goals have remained through its fourth generation.

7020 NY-89, Ovid, NY 14521

Red Newt Cellars - Seneca Lake

Red Newt Cellars is located just outside the bucolic hamlet of Hector on the east side of Seneca Lake in the Finger Lakes wine region of New York. As a recipient of Wine & Spirits Magazine’s 2011 Winery of the Year, the message at Red Newt Cellars is simple: grown here, made here.

3675 Tichenor Rd, Hector, NY 14841
